Pre-workshop

Record your radio intro

According to NPR, your intro can make or break your story and they are difficult to write. We're going to kick off this workshop with a bang by asking you to record your own radio intro.

Before you begin, get your recording gear in order. This could be a smartphone or USB microphone. Smartphones have access to Voice Memo (iPhone) or Audio Recorder (Android) software. You will also want to download your recording software of choice. We recommend Audacity.

If using Audacity, install the LAME MP3 Encoder to export MP3 files. You can view a tutorial for this installation on YouTube..

Using the following script, insert your unique identifiers into the blank:

Hello, and welcome to the UW Libraries Podcasting workshop. This is name reporting for department. I'm here today because reason why you joined this workshop. For more on this story, stay tuned to the UW Libraries Podcasting workshop for details.

Record yourself reading your script and put it together in Audacity. You may want to check out a beginners tutorial or go directly to Audacity Manual to learn how to import audio files into your project.

Export your audio file as an MP3. Remember, you need to download and install the LAME MP3 Encoder if you are using Audacity.

Upload your MP3 file to your unique Slack channel.

This assignment is due by the first workshop session.
